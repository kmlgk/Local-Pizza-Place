The Secret to a Perfect Wood-Fired Crust

Marco Belli

Jun 18, 2026· 6 min read

Ask ten pizzaiolos what makes a great crust and you'll get ten answers. After fifteen years and a few thousand pies, here's what actually matters in our kitchen.

Start with a slow, cold ferment

Our dough rests for 24 hours in the walk-in before it ever sees the oven. That slow fermentation is what gives the crust its flavor and those big, irregular air bubbles you want in the cornicione.

Stretch by hand, never with a rolling pin

A rolling pin crushes out all the air you just spent a day building up. Hand-stretching from the center out keeps the crust light and preserves the crumb structure.

900°F changes everything

Our wood-fired oven runs at nearly 900°F — hot enough to cook a pizza in 90 seconds. That speed is what gives you a crust that's crisp on the outside and still soft inside, without drying out the toppings.

"You can't rush good dough. Everything else follows from getting that part right."

Getting close at home

  • Let your dough cold-ferment in the fridge for at least 24 hours.
  • Preheat a pizza stone or steel for a full hour before baking.
  • Stretch by hand and resist the urge to overload with toppings.
